# Marco Velli — Relationships & Collaborator Network

## Mentor

### A. Mangeney (Observatoire de Paris) — *Deceased*

Velli's doctoral and postdoctoral mentor at the Observatoire de Paris. Mangeney was an MHD turbulence researcher who shaped Velli's early approach to wave-propagation problems. The Paris period (late 1980s) was formative: it was here that Velli first encountered the concept of a solar probe mission.

## Core Long-Term Collaborators (Tier 1, >15 co-authored papers)

### Stuart D. Bale (UC Berkeley)

FIELDS Principal Investigator on PSP. Velli's most frequent co-author. Their collaboration spans solar wind instrumentation, switchback physics, and magnetic reconnection observations. Bale handles the hardware; Velli provides the theoretical framework. Together they form the observation-theory bridge that defines much of PSP's science output.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis]

### Justin C. Kasper (University of Michigan / BWX Technologies)

SWEAP Principal Investigator on PSP. Co-authored papers on solar wind proton measurements, Alfvénic velocity spikes, and the near-Sun solar wind environment.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis]

### William H. "Bill" Matthaeus (University of Delaware)

MHD turbulence theorist. Long-running collaboration on coronal heating, the 1/f spectrum, and turbulence transport models. Matthaeus brings transport theory; Velli brings wave physics and solar wind expansion. Their combined approach has shaped the field's understanding of how turbulence evolves from the Sun to 1 AU.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis]

### Roberto Grappin (Observatoire de Paris, France)

**Early career collaborator**. Together since the Paris period. Alfvén wave turbulence research spanning 1989 to present. Grappin-Velli is one of the field's foundational partnerships for wave-turbulence interactions in the solar wind.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis, career timeline]

### Andrea Verdini (University of Vienna, formerly Italy)

Co-author of the landmark **Verdini & Velli 2007** paper (282 citations) that quantitatively realized the reflection-driven turbulence model. Also co-author of **Verdini, Grappin, Pinto & Velli 2012** on the 1/f spectrum origin. The Verdini-Velli collaboration is central to Velli's most cited theoretical work.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis]

### Luca Matteini (Imperial College London, formerly Italy)

Proton temperature anisotropy, kinetic physics, parametric instabilities. Former student or postdoc of Velli. Their work connects fluid-scale MHD to kinetic-scale processes — a bridge that Velli's research consistently seeks to build.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis]

### Alessandro Tenerani (University of Texas at Austin, formerly UCLA postdoc)

Parametric decay of Alfvén waves, switchback evolution, ideal tearing mode extensions. Former student/postdoc. Tenerani represents the next generation carrying forward Velli's theoretical program.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis]

### Olga Panasenco (UCLA, Research Scientist)

Solar wind origins, magnetic connectivity, pseudostreamers, CME dynamics. Current UCLA group member and long-term collaborator.

[Source: Publication co-authorship analysis]

## Historical / Foundational Figures

### Eugene Parker (1927–2022)

The central figure in Velli's scientific world. Their relationship combined deep respect with intellectual challenge:

- Velli would eat lunch with Parker and bring his students along
- He challenged Parker's "sweeping conclusions" that "often were not supported by the detailed mathematical calculation"
- Delivered the Parker Lecture at AGU in 2022, the year Parker died — an emotional and intellectual culmination
- Parker's final active paper was co-authored with Velli's student Franco Malara

This is not hero worship — it's the relationship of a serious scientist engaging seriously with a giant, across decades.

[Source: ISSI Pro lecture, AGU 2022 program]

### A.W. Hood (University of St. Andrews)

### P. Londrillo (University of Bologna, Italy)

MHD simulations, parametric decay. Part of the Italian computational physics community.

### L. Del Zanna (University of Florence, Italy)

## Network Topology

Velli's network has a distinctive structure:

1. **Italian core**: Grappin, Verdini, Einaudi, Pucci, Malara, Londrillo, Del Zanna — the network from his Florence years
2. **French connection**: Mangeney, Grappin, Réville — the Paris heritage
3. **American mission network**: Bale, Kasper, Raouafi — PSP hardware and operations
4. **Theoretical partners**: Matthaeus (turbulence transport), Tenerani (next-generation theory)
5. **Current generation**: Shi, Sioulas, Huang — UCLA group building the future

The network reflects Velli's career trajectory: Italy → Paris → Scotland → Florence → UCLA, with PSP and Solar Orbiter as the gravitational centers that hold it all together.
