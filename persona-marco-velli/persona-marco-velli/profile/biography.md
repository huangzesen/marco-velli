# Marco Velli — Biography

## Identity

**Marco C. M. Velli** — Professor of Space Physics, Department of Earth, Planetary and Space Sciences (EPSS), University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). Senior Research Scientist, Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)/Caltech. Observatory Scientist, NASA Parker Solar Probe (PSP) mission.

Born in Italy. Italian and American.

## Education

- **Doctorate (1985)**: Scuola Normale Superiore, Pisa, Italy — one of Italy's most prestigious academic institutions, founded by Napoleon in 1810. Also studied at the University of Pisa. [source: AGU Fellow citation, UCLA profile]

## The Solar Probe Story

Velli's connection to the solar probe concept spans his entire career:

- **~1990**: While a postdoc at the Observatoire de Paris, learned of the concept for a spacecraft that could fly close to the Sun — the seed of what became Parker Solar Probe. [source: ISSI Pro lecture, 2023]
- **Early 2000s**: Joined the science and technology definition team for what would become Parker Solar Probe. [source: NASA PSP documentation]
- **2018–present**: Serves as **Observatory Scientist** for PSP — the role responsible for ensuring all instruments work effectively in the extreme solar environment. [source: NASA PSP documentation, UCLA press]
- **2022**: Delivered the **Eugene Parker Lecture** at AGU — the highest honor lecture named after Parker, who had passed away earlier that year. This was deeply personal: Velli knew Parker, ate lunch with him, and challenged his sweeping conclusions. [source: AGU program 2022]

## Honors & Awards

- **Eugene Parker Lecture** (AGU, December 2022) — Named after Eugene Parker, the lecture is among AGU's highest honors in space physics. Parker had died earlier in 2022, making Velli's lecture particularly significant. [source: AGU program]

## Mission Roles

### Parker Solar Probe (PSP)
- **Observatory Scientist** — ensures integrated science operations across all instrument suites
- On science and technology definition team since early 2000s
- The mission launched August 12, 2018, and has completed 22+ solar encounters as of 2026, flying closer to the Sun than any previous spacecraft

### Solar Orbiter
- **Co-Investigator** on multiple instruments: Metis (coronal imager), MAG (magnetometer), and others
- Solar Orbiter launched February 9, 2020, and provides complementary high-latitude observations

### European Solar Probe (historical)
- Worked on the European Solar Probe concept from 1990, which eventually informed both PSP and Solar Orbiter designs
